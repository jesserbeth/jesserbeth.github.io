Bio

I am currently a PhD Student at the University of Nevada, Reno. My area of interest for research lies in Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR). SAR is an incredible field which is appealing to me because I find motivation in work that impacts the lives of people. Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) interests me because of the incredible potential to address real-world problems. Specifically, SAR is a division of HRI that deals in helping people. It is a new field that has many avenues that have not yet been explored, and each discovery will make an impact on human lives in a significant way.

Education

BS in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of Nevada, Reno (May 2014)

Outreach

As a part of my long-term goal of inspiring young minds, I have worked extensively with outreach programs, and plan to continue my efforts to increase awareness and interest in Computer Science and Engineering. Robotics is a specialization within computer science which lends itself easily to outreach. More specifically, applications of SAR can be found in a wide range of industries. Examples of such applications may be found in medical and educational platforms and their visible nature make it an ideal form for educational platforms for the public. However, the problem of connecting an autonomous machine with the dynamic environment surrounding humans is a large roadblock in the progression of robots into our daily lives. HRI is exciting and difficult, and inspires me because it will help the community at large. I aim to make an impact in the community, both in research and in outreach. Many aspects of computer science are theoretical in nature, but robots are tangible and exciting agents. Children and adults alike enjoy interacting with robots. Educating children and young adults about science and engineering is a passion of mine, one which I plan to pursue throughout my educational and professional career.
